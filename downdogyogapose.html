Alternate Nostril Breathing (Nadi Shodhana)

Follow these steps to begin:

  • Sit comfortably with your spine straight.
  • Close your right nostril with your thumb and inhale deeply through your left nostril.
  • Close your left nostril with your ring finger, release your right nostril, and exhale slowly.
  • Inhale through your right nostril, then switch and exhale through your left nostril.
  • Repeat for a few minutes, focusing on your breath.

Learn Yoga: Downward Dog Pose

Step-by-Step Instructions

The Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana) pose is a foundational pose in yoga that stretches and strengthens the body. It is great for relieving tension and increasing flexibility.

How to Perform Downward Dog:

  • Step 1: Start on your hands and knees, with your wrists aligned under your shoulders and your knees under your hips.
  • Step 2: Spread your fingers wide and press firmly into the ground. Tuck your toes under.
  • Step 3: As you exhale, lift your hips toward the ceiling, aiming to create an upside-down V-shape with your body.

Tips for Proper Alignment:

  • Make sure your hands are shoulder-width apart and your feet are hip-width apart.
  • Engage your core to avoid collapsing your lower back.
  • Look between your legs or at your belly button, keeping your neck in a neutral position.

Practice regularly to feel the full benefits of this pose, including improved flexibility, posture, and strength.
